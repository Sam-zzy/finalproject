Benign Tumors

Since benign tumors pose no serious health risks, physicians may choose to monitor the lump instead of performing a surgical removal. However, if you feel discomfort, irritation, or a sense of pressure, be sure to inform your doctor so that appropriate steps can be taken to remove the growth and enhance your well-being.

Malignant tumors

Malignant tumors are dangerous and fast-growing, often spreading into surrounding tissues and causing extensive harm. When a tumor is believed to be malignant, a healthcare provider usually conducts a biopsy to examine its characteristics, determine its severity, and assess how aggressively it may progress.
